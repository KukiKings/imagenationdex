## What IN$DEX Is Registering As

Under the new AML/CTF laws (in force 1 July 2026), IN$DEX provides **two categories** of regulated designated services:

1. **Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP)** — crypto-to-crypto DEX, INDX token exchange, wallet services, staking
2. **Remittance Service Provider (RSP)** — Pacific corridors (Melbourne ↔ Samoa, Fiji, Vanuatu, RMI)

Both require enrolment + registration. You must register as both.

## Two-Step Process

### Step 1 — Enrol (do this first, today)
1. Go to **https://online.austrac.gov.au**
2. Create a user account (or log in if you already have one)
3. Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA required)
4. Go to **Business menu → Enrol a new business**
5. Complete the enrolment form (you can save and return for up to 14 days)
6. Submit → you receive an **AUSTRAC Account Number (AAN)**

### Step 2 — Register (immediately after enrolment)
After enrolling, AUSTRAC emails you a **PDF registration form**.
Complete it and return it. They then assess your application (up to 90 days).

## Documents to Prepare Before Submitting Registration Form

After enrolment, the registration form asks you to demonstrate you can manage ML/TF risks. Prepare these now:

### 1. AML/CTF Program — Part A (Risk & Governance)
This is the biggest document. It must cover:
- [ ] **Business Risk Assessment** — types of ML/TF risks your customers/products/corridors present
- [ ] **Board/Senior Management oversight** structure for AML/CTF
- [ ] **Transaction monitoring** procedures — how you detect suspicious activity
- [ ] **Suspicious Matter Reporting (SMR)** procedures — how and when you report to AUSTRAC
- [ ] **Threshold Transaction Reports (TTR)** — AUD $10,000+ cash transactions
- [ ] **International Funds Transfer Instruction (IFTI)** reporting — every cross-border transfer
- [ ] **Enhanced Customer Due Diligence (ECDD)** — for high-risk customers/corridors
- [ ] **Sanctions screening** procedures (OFAC, UN, Australian list)
- [ ] **Correspondent relationships** — due diligence on payment partners
- [ ] **Staff training program** — how all staff are trained on AML/CTF

### 2. AML/CTF Program — Part B (KYC / Customer Identification)
- [ ] **Customer Identification Procedure (CIP)** — how you identify citizens at onboarding
- [ ] **KYC tiers** — what ID is collected at each tier (phone-only, basic KYC, enhanced KYC)
- [ ] **Ongoing Customer Due Diligence (OCDD)** — how you monitor customers over time
- [ ] **Politically Exposed Persons (PEP)** screening procedure
- [ ] **Adverse media screening** procedure
- [ ] **Wallet due diligence** — counterparty wallet screening for VASP transfers

### 3. Travel Rule Compliance (mandatory from 1 July 2026)
For every virtual asset transfer, you must collect and transmit:
- [ ] Sender full name + wallet address
- [ ] Receiver full name + wallet address
- [ ] Amount (in virtual asset + AUD equivalent)
- [ ] Unique transaction reference / hash
- [ ] Exchange rate used
Document how your platform collects and transmits this data.

### 4. Business Description
- [ ] Plain-language description of what IN$DEX does
- [ ] Customer types (retail citizens, Pacific diaspora, builders)
- [ ] Products and services (DEX, remittance, staking, INDX token, Grid Account)
- [ ] Technology (Solana blockchain, Supabase backend, Phantom wallet integration)
- [ ] How transactions flow through the platform
- [ ] Estimated monthly transaction volumes (AUD)

### 5. Key Personnel Profiles
For AJ + any other key management:
- [ ] CV / résumé showing relevant experience
- [ ] Compliance/AML/CTF experience (if any)
- [ ] Prior directorships
- [ ] Clean criminal history declaration

### 6. Record-Keeping Policy
- [ ] Confirm records kept for **7 years** minimum
- [ ] What records are kept (transaction records, customer ID, SMRs, TTRs, IFTIs)
- [ ] Where records are stored (Supabase, on-chain, etc.)
