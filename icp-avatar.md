# ICP Avatar — Mama Noe

## Who She Is

**Name:** Mama Noe
**Age:** 80
**Location:** Mele Village, Vanuatu
**Occupation:** Street food vendor — maker and seller of *tuluk* (grated cassava stuffed with seasoned meat, wrapped in banana leaves, baked in an umu)

## Her Daily Life

Mama Noe is a matriarch. Every week, her grandchildren gather at her home to help her prepare tuluk. It's not just a business — it's a family ritual. When the snacks are ready, she carries them to the local general store in Mele Village and sets up her table out front, selling each tuluk for $1.50 cash.

She goes to church every week without fail. Her earnings go two places: tithes and offerings to her church, and payment to her grandchildren for their help. Every dollar she makes is already spoken for by people and purposes she loves.

## Her Relationship with Technology

Her children taught her to use her smartphone. She uses it for Facebook, calls, and photos — about 50% confident. She's not a tech native, but she's not afraid of it either. If something is simple enough and someone she trusts shows her once, she'll use it.

## Her Problem

Mama Noe only accepts cash. That single constraint caps her income. Tourists, younger locals, and digital-first customers walk past because they're not carrying coins. She can't take their money. She has no bank account, no card terminal, no way to receive digital payment.

She is not unambitious. She is unequipped.
