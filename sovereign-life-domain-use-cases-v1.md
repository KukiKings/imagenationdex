# IN$DEX Sovereign Life Domain — God Mode Use-Case Architecture

> Canonical spec — authored by AJ, saved 2026-07-15. Companion/expansion document to `sovereign-domain-credential-layer-v1.md` (saved earlier this session). 140 numbered use cases across 18 parts. Nothing here is built yet — see build-status notes at the bottom before starting any implementation work.

## Definition

The IN$DEX Sovereign Life Domain is the citizen-controlled gateway connecting verified identity, credentials, capabilities, work, business, ownership, commerce, finance, contribution, culture, governance, protection, and legacy.

Example: `yourname.IN$DEX`

It replaces fragmented presentation. It does not replace lawful authority. It connects trusted proof from authorised issuers. It reveals only what a transaction requires. It turns verified history into future opportunity. It remains portable beyond IN$DEX.

The domain represents the citizen. The domain does not own or define the citizen.

## Core experience

The citizen speaks. SIINDEX understands the purpose. SIINDEX identifies the required proof. SIINDEX checks the requester. SIINDEX selects the minimum information. SIINDEX explains the consequences. The citizen approves. IN$DEX provides the proof. IN$DEX records the consent receipt. The citizen remains in control.

## PART ONE — Sovereign Identity Use Cases

**1. Reusable Identity Verification** — the citizen verifies identity once; future institutions request verified attributes (identity verified, age threshold satisfied, citizenship verified, residency verified, address verified, legal name verified, business role verified, guardian status verified) rather than repeated document copies. Receiving organisations may still perform additional checks where law or policy requires.

**2. Minimum Disclosure Identity** — proves one required fact without exposing unrelated information (over 18 without full DOB, resident of an eligible region without public address, Cook Islands citizen without sharing a passport copy, licensed driver without exposing the full licence record, authorised director without exposing private identity documents).

**3. Root Identity Recovery** — a lost phone, forgotten password, compromised key, or destroyed device does not destroy identity. Recovery may use trusted recovery contacts, government identity checks, institutional verification, biometric verification where lawful, recovery codes, hardware security keys, community verification, guardian verification, delayed recovery review.

**4. Name and Identity History** — legal name, chosen name, cultural name, professional name, business name, former name, public display name, private legal identity. Citizens don't need to publish their legal name unless required.

**5. Family and Household Credentials** — parent/guardian authority, emergency contact, dependent relationship, caregiver authority, household membership, authorised pickup rights, family business role, estate beneficiary status. Sensitive family information stays permission-controlled.

**6. Delegate Authority** — citizen grants limited authority to accountant, lawyer, business administrator, carer, parent, guardian, employee, cultural representative, project manager, recovery contact — each delegation states allowed/prohibited actions, visible information, approval limits, duration, revocation rights.

**7. Emergency Identity Card** — offline emergency proof with only approved essential info: name, emergency contact, allergy alert, medical risk alert, guardian contact, blood type where medically confirmed, language needs, accessibility needs, emergency support instructions.

**8. Anonymous and Pseudonymous Participation** — protected identity for sensitive support groups, whistleblowing, governance consultation, community complaints, creative work, early business exploration, safety reporting. IN$DEX retains lawful escalation for serious harm, fraud, or court orders.

## PART SEVENTEEN — System-Wide Non-Negotiable Rules

The domain must use open, interoperable credential standards. The citizen must be able to export their records. The domain must support selective disclosure. The public profile must remain separate from Root Identity. Credentials must identify their issuer and evidence. The wallet must remain separable from public identity. Qualifications must remain separate from INDX rewards. Essential identity access must never require token ownership.

No universal social score. No speculative credential trading. No public child profile by default. No public wallet balance by default. No hidden institutional access. No permanent consent by default. No data sale. No political profiling. No cultural disclosure without authority. No automated final decision for serious citizen harm without human review. No domain suspension without due process. No wallet failure should destroy credentials. No SIINDEX failure should erase citizen access. No IN$DEX failure should trap citizen records.

The citizen retains the right to recover, correct, challenge, export, revoke, and leave.

## Final Position

A CV describes the citizen. A certificate records one achievement. A wallet holds selected credentials and assets. A website presents information. A business card creates an introduction. KYC confirms required identity facts.

The IN$DEX Sovereign Life Domain connects all of them. It proves the citizen's verified past. It protects the citizen's present. It prepares the citizen's next opportunity. It connects learning to capability. Capability to evidence. Evidence to trust. Trust to opportunity. Opportunity to income. Income to assets. Assets to ownership. Ownership to resilience. Resilience to legacy.

Your IN$DEX domain does not sit online waiting to be viewed. SIINDEX helps it work for you.

One citizen. One sovereign gateway. Many verified roles. Minimum disclosure. Continuous opportunity. Protected ownership. Permanent portability. Living legacy.

## Build status (added by Claude, not part of AJ's original spec)

This is a north-star vision document — 140 use cases spanning identity, education, employment, business, marketplace, finance, government, health, travel, culture, disaster response, IP, property, trust/safety, opportunity-matching, and institutional integration. None of it is built. Almost none of the underlying infrastructure it assumes exists yet either (no credential-issuance system, no institutional-issuer model, no selective-disclosure engine, no multi-view domain rendering).

Current platform reality, for scale comparison: `citizens.web3_domain` is a single flat string per citizen. `consent_receipts` (built this session) is the closest existing primitive to "consent receipt" / credential-use-receipt described throughout this doc. KYC today is Tier 0 only (phone + liveness scan, no documents, no third-party institutional verification of any kind).

Given the scope, this reads as multi-year platform doctrine (on the order of the whitepaper itself) rather than a single build task. Recommend treating this and `sovereign-domain-credential-layer-v1.md` together as the reference doctrine for a future dedicated roadmap phase, and picking one narrow, real slice to prototype first if/when AJ wants to start building against it — rather than attempting to scope the whole thing as one task.
