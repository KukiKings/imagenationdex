## The Three Brains

### Brain 1: Identity Brain

Defines who the citizen is, who they serve, and how they operate.

**Inputs:** Name / story / skills / values / culture / community / existing assets / trust proof

**Outputs:**
1. Positioning statement
2. One-line identity
3. Audience definition
4. Voice rules (what to say, what never to say)
5. Visual direction
6. Values
7. Trust proof
8. Cultural status + permissions
9. Safety boundaries
10. Forbidden claims
11. Life Graph tags

---

### Brain 2: Opportunity Brain

Defines what the citizen should sell, to whom, at what price — and what weak ideas should die.

**Inputs:** Identity Brain + citizen's available assets, time, capital, cultural context

**Process:**
- Generate 3 offer directions
- For each: name, type, buyer, price range, why it wins, why it may fail, required assets, trust/cultural/child safety requirements, local/global/marketplace/live commerce/learning/ownership path
- **Weak Idea Killer:** argue like an investor, eliminate weak ideas, explain why
- Select best first offer based on: easiest first action, lowest risk, clearest buyer, strongest trust fit, fastest path to first sale, long-term potential, cultural safety, citizen capability gain

**Outputs:**
1. Best offer (selected)
2. Why this offer wins
3. Why rejected ideas were weaker
4. Pricing logic
5. First customer path
6. Marketplace listing plan
7. Trust checklist
8. Revenue paths (local / global / live / licensing / learning)
9. 14-day action plan
10. Long-term expansion path

## Example: Vanuatu Prints

**Citizen input:** "I want to create income from handmade Vanuatu prints."

### Identity Brain
- **Positioning:** A Vanuatu print creator turning traditional-inspired designs, island stories, and handmade craft into protected, beautiful products for tourists, diaspora families, collectors, and culture-conscious buyers.
- **Audience:** Tourists, Pacific diaspora, gift buyers, ethical fashion buyers, local hotels, cultural event organizers.
- **Voice:** Warm, proud, respectful, story-led, simple, culturally careful.
- **Visual direction:** Natural textures, island color, close-up craft process, maker hands, pattern meaning, product in real-life settings.
- **Trust proof:** Creator identity, product photos, process proof, cultural permission status, buyer protection, local pickup/shipping details.
- **Cultural boundary:** Any traditional motifs must be classified before commercial use.

### Opportunity Brain

**Offer 1: Handmade Print Product Pack**
- Physical product
- Best for tourists and local buyers
- Fastest first sale
- Lower cultural complexity if designs are approved
- ✅ Recommended first offer

**Offer 2: Story + Print Gift Bundle**
- Higher value
- Includes printed cultural story card
- Stronger tourist appeal
- Requires accurate attribution and permission

**Offer 3: Digital Licensing Pack**
- Potentially scalable
- Higher risk
- Requires strict cultural IP approval
- ✕ Not first move

**Selected offer:** Handmade Print Product Pack + Story Card Bundle
**Why it wins:** Easy to understand, photograph, and sell locally. Strong emotional value. Can grow into online sales.

### Content Brain — 5 Pillars
1. **The Print Has a Story** — Cultural meaning, process, inspiration, respectful education.
2. **From Hands to Home** — Behind-the-scenes creation and product use.
3. **Vanuatu Gift With Meaning** — Tourism/gift angle.
4. **Protect Culture, Don't Copy It** — Cultural permission and attribution.
5. **This Week's Print Drop** — Product and sales posts.

### Opportunity Magnet
**Title:** "5 Vanuatu Print Stories Every Visitor Should Know"
**Format:** Free visual guide / WhatsApp download / email PDF.
**Purpose:** Build trust, educate buyers, collect interested customers.

### 14-Day Launch Plan
- Day 1: Photograph 5 products
- Day 2: Confirm cultural permission status
- Day 3: Create IN$DEX opportunity page
- Day 4: Publish first listing
- Day 5: Post story carousel
- Day 6: Create WhatsApp buyer message
- Day 7: Run first mini live commerce session
- Day 8: Share buyer FAQ
- Day 9: Offer bundle
- Day 10: Contact hotels/tour guides
- Day 11: Publish customer story if consented
- Day 12: Improve listing
- Day 13: Launch second print drop
- Day 14: Review signals and update Brain Passport
