## The 9-Layer Architecture

### Layer 1 — Interface Layer
SIINDEX visual body, citizen dashboard, Mission Room panel, mobile voice button.
The citizen's window into SIINDEX. Every command begins here.

### Layer 2 — Voice Layer
Speech-to-text (Web Speech API, 18 languages from Jun 2026), text-to-speech (SpeechSynthesis, female sovereign voice), push-to-talk mode, approved SIINDEX voice modes (calm, clear, warm, non-technical).

### Layer 3 — Synthetic Intelligence Layer
SIINDEX reasoning engine: mission identification, intent parsing, context reading from Brain Passport, Life Graph, Mission Rooms, Decision Ledger. Safety check before every step. Memory across sessions.

### Layer 4 — Tool Layer
Approved connectors only:
- Calendar (read/write)
- Files (read, prepare, never delete without approval)
- Email / Messages (read, draft — never send without approval)
- Marketplace (read, prepare listing — never publish without approval)
- Social Scheduler (draft, schedule — publish requires approval)
- Design Tools (generate, prepare — never commit without approval)
- Payments / Payouts (read, calculate — never initiate without approval)
- Governance (read proposals, prepare votes — never submit without approval)
- Mission Rooms (read, update, prepare decisions)
- Security / Session (read, alert — never change settings without approval)
- Data Vault (read with consent, never export without approval)
- Cultural Rights Graph (read, check clearance, never commercialize without approval)

### Layer 5 — Permission Layer
Each tool has 5 scopes:
- **Read** — SIINDEX can see it
- **Prepare** — SIINDEX can draft/calculate/plan
- **Write** — SIINDEX can save internally (not publish)
- **Execute** — SIINDEX can act (requires mission approval)
- **Revoke** — Citizen can remove at any time, instantly

### Layer 6 — Secret Management Layer
API keys, private keys, passwords, session tokens, and all credentials must be stored in secure secret management (not inside prompts, chat messages, files, logs, or public outputs). SIINDEX never sees raw credentials.

### Layer 7 — Ledger Layer
Every command, every action, every preparation, every approval, every refusal — logged.
- **Decision Ledger**: what was decided and by whom
- **Artifact Ledger**: what was created, drafted, or prepared
- **Security Ledger**: what was accessed, when, and under what permission

### Layer 8 — Consent Layer
Sensitive actions require **explicit citizen approval** before execution.

**Sensitive actions requiring approval:**
- Moving money or initiating payouts
- Changing payout details or wallet addresses
- Connecting external wallets
- Publishing content externally
- Sending messages to third parties
- Creating or modifying marketplace listings (live)
- Sharing any private citizen data
- Signing agreements or consenting to terms
- Commercializing cultural assets
- Exposing any child-related information
- Voting on or submitting governance proposals
- Connecting new external tools
- Deleting, exporting, or transferring important records
- Changing security or recovery settings
- Executing financial trades

### Layer 9 — Shutdown / Pause Layer
**"SIINDEX, stop all actions."**

The citizen has a master kill switch at all times. It must:
- Terminate the current session scope immediately (not credential revocation — session-scoped)
- Signal all in-flight tool calls to abort
- Flush the task queue
- Preserve the execution trace up to termination
- Revoke any active write/execute permissions instantly
- Require explicit re-authorisation to resume

*This is EU AI Act compliant and aligned with Singapore IMDA 2026 agentic AI governance standards.*

## The Daily Brief Format

Delivered every morning when the citizen opens the app. Auto-read aloud. 7 sections:

1. **Greeting** — personalised, time-aware, warm sovereign tone
2. **Highest-priority mission** — the one thing that needs attention most today
3. **Pending approvals** — what SIINDEX prepared and is waiting for citizen sign-off
4. **Citizen opportunities** — buyer questions, collab requests, governance votes, payout ready
5. **Risks or protections needed** — compliance alerts, security notices, expiring deadlines
6. **Suggested next best action** — SIINDEX's recommendation (not an instruction)
7. **Ask what the citizen wants to do** — hand control back to the citizen

**Example brief:**
> "Good morning, citizen. Your marketplace listing has two buyer questions waiting. Your Instagram reel is scheduled for 5 p.m. One payout requires your confirmation. Your Mission Room has one decision open. Your AUSTRAC enrollment is due in 19 days — I have the checklist ready. Would you like me to draft replies, review the payout, or open your Mission Room first?"
