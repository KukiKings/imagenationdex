## The Unhackable Login — Biometric Sovereign Credential

AJ's question: "What do I actually use to log in? Is it password? Voice? Retina? NFT of my face? What cannot be hacked?"

### The Login Stack

**Layer 1 — Active Biometric Liveness**
Your face, fingerprint, or iris scans live on your phone's hardware security chip (Apple Secure Enclave or Android StrongBox). Not on IN$DEX servers. Not in any database. The chip receives a random liveness challenge on every login — "blink left eye, then smile" — that changes every session. **Layer 2 — Passkey Private Key (Stays in Hardware Forever)**
Inside the same hardware chip is your FIDO2 passkey private key — a cryptographic key that has never been transmitted anywhere, not even during setup. When your biometric passes, the chip uses this key to sign a one-time challenge from IN$DEX. The signature goes back. IN$DEX verifies it against your public key on Solana under `yourname.IN$DEX`. The private key did not move. There is nothing on any server for a hacker to steal.

**Layer 3 — Biometric Sovereign Credential (BSC)**

At first enrolment, a ZK proof is generated: *"live human, unique biometric, liveness confirmed, deepfake test passed."* That proof — not the face data — is stored as a Verifiable Credential on Solana via the Solana Attestation Service. The BSC is:
- **Soulbound:** cannot be transferred, sold, or duplicated
- **ZK-only:** the proof proves identity; the actual biometric never leaves the device
- **Renewable:** re-verification issues a fresh BSC with new timestamp
- **Checked on high-value actions:** payout approval, governance vote, guardian change

The BSC is not a photo. It is not a face NFT in the speculative sense. It is a cryptographic, on-chain proof that a live, unique human — verified by hardware challenge-response — enacted this identity at this moment.

## Smart Wallet Foundation — ERC-4337 / EIP-7702

`yourname.IN$DEX` anchors to a **smart wallet** (ERC-4337 smart account), not a bare keypair (Externally Owned Account / EOA). This is the architectural decision that unlocks every other sovereign feature.

**Why EOAs are insufficient:**
An EOA is a raw private key. If you lose the key, funds are gone permanently. No recovery. No programmable logic. No session control. No guardian mechanism at the protocol level.

**What the ERC-4337 smart wallet unlocks:**
- **Social guardian recovery in contract logic** — the guardian threshold (3-of-5) is enforced by code, not by trusting IN$DEX to honour it
- **Session keys** — limited-permission temporary keys for specific tasks. A session key for marketplace purchases cannot drain the full wallet. A session key for an agent cannot trigger governance

By 2026, there are 200+ million smart wallets deployed across EVM chains. EIP-7702 (activated in Ethereum's Pectra upgrade, May 2025) extended this to existing wallets. The smart wallet is no longer experimental — it is the mainstream infrastructure.

Every feature of the `yourname.IN$DEX` Verification Method Stack maps to a smart wallet module:
- Passkey → replaces seed phrase as the signing key

**Biometric gate:** The Biometric Sovereign Credential (BSC) requires active liveness from a real human face. One face = one BSC = one identity. Creating a second identity requires a second face passing active liveness detection. This is not a technical exploit — it requires another real person.

**Behavioral biometrics:** Interaction patterns, typing rhythms, and device motion profiles make impersonation detectable over time even without explicit biometric checks.

**Governance implication:** One `yourname.IN$DEX` = one governance vote. Sybil resistance of the identity layer IS the democratic integrity of the IN$DEX civilization. You cannot buy more votes by creating more wallets. You cannot manufacture consensus by spinning up more addresses.
