2. **Progressive KYC alignment.** The ramp must support tiered or reliance-based KYC — accepting IN$DEX's Tier 0 biometric liveness scan as sufficient verification for small transactions. Demanding document KYC for every transaction defeats the purpose of banking the unbanked.
3. **Pacific coverage.** The ramp must operate in or near the Pacific — Cook Islands, PNG, Fiji, Tonga, Samoa, Vanuatu, and the Filipino diaspora corridor.
4. **Solana support.** The ramp must support SPL tokens on Solana mainnet.
5. **Transparent policy.** KYC thresholds, blocked regions, and appeal processes must be publicly documented and accessible.

Transak is the recommended primary ramp for IN$DEX. Their **KYC Reliance** feature is the critical differentiator: Transak accepts IN$DEX's existing biometric KYC via Sumsub integration, meaning a citizen who completed IN$DEX Tier 0 onboarding does not face a second KYC challenge at the ramp. Kado serves as the preferred widget for Solana-specific flows and as a backup if Transak coverage gaps exist in specific Pacific territories.

MoonPay fails criteria 1 and 2. Their KYC model requires document verification that conflicts with IN$DEX's Tier 0 progressive biometric approach.
