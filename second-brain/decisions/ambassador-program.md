## Why Ambassadors — Not Influencers

The data is clear: 45% more engagement from 5K–30K micro-voices than from mega-influencers. But for IN$DEX, the reason goes deeper than that.

The unbanked don't trust platforms. They trust people. GoPay and GCash scaled in Southeast Asia because they moved through ride-hailing drivers and market sellers — people the community already knew. IN$DEX scales through the same model. The ambassador IS the product at the street level.

Mama Noe doesn't need to understand DeFi. She needs to trust the person showing her.

That's it. No sales scripts. No pressure. Genuine onboarding.

## Pacific Islands Targeting Strategy

**The wedge market is clear from the data:**
- Pacific Islands are mobile-first, high community trust, low banking penetration
- Remittances are a massive pain point — Pacific diaspora sending money home pays 10–15% fees
- IN$DEX + Civ Law = 2% for the same corridor. That is the sell.
- Church networks are the distribution layer — one pastor = hundreds of families

**Priority communities:**
1. **Samoan diaspora (NZ/Australia → Samoa)** — large remittance corridor, tech-comfortable
2. **Tongan communities (NZ/Australia → Tonga)** — similar profile, tight-knit
3. **Fijian Facebook groups** — highly active, mobile-first
4. **PNG urban centres (Port Moresby, Lae)** — young, mobile, underbanked
5. **Solomon Islands** — CBDC pilot underway — IN$DEX can position as the citizen alternative

**Outreach approach:**
- Facebook groups first (that's where Pacific communities actually are)
- WhatsApp voice notes — better than text for lower-literacy contexts
- Church leader partnerships — get one pastor on board per island group
- Market seller demo days — set up a table, show people live
