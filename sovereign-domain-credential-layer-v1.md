# IN$DEX Sovereign Domain and Credential Layer

> Canonical spec — authored by AJ, saved 2026-07-15. Not yet built. See build-status notes at the bottom before starting any implementation work.

## Purpose

Replace fragmented identity presentation with one citizen-controlled sovereign domain.

Every citizen may receive a unique IN$DEX domain.

Example: `yourname.in$dex`

The domain becomes the citizen's permanent address inside the civilisation. It connects identity, credentials, capabilities, ownership, reputation, commerce, contribution, and opportunity through one trusted interface.

## Core doctrine

One citizen. One sovereign address. Many verified roles.

The citizen controls access. SIINDEX explains every request. IN$DEX records every permission.

The domain does not replace the citizen. The domain represents the citizen's approved proof.

## The New Sovereign Identity Standard

For years, citizens relied on disconnected tools. A CV described employment history. A résumé promoted capability. A business card shared contact details. A certificate showed education. A portfolio showed work. References described reputation. KYC documents proved legal identity. A website showed public information. A wallet held digital assets. A social profile showed activity. A marketplace profile showed products.

Each system held a separate version of the person. Each institution asked the citizen to start again.

IN$DEX connects these functions through one citizen-controlled domain. The citizen no longer sends ten disconnected records. The citizen shares the correct permissioned view of `yourname.in$dex`.

## Sovereign Domain Functions

The domain may serve as: citizen identity page, digital business card, living CV, verified résumé, capability portfolio, credential wallet, qualification record, project history, employment history, business profile, marketplace page, creator page, mentor profile, student profile, community contribution record, professional licence directory, cultural permission record, reputation profile, booking page, payment route, communication address, governance profile, ownership record, consent centre, opportunity gateway.

## Living CV

The traditional CV is static. It becomes outdated. Claims are difficult to verify. References remain disconnected. Work evidence often disappears.

The IN$DEX Living CV updates through verified citizen activity. It may include: verified employment, completed projects, qualifications, micro-credentials, demonstrated capabilities, employer confirmations, client confirmations, mentor endorsements, community service, business experience, marketplace performance, languages, cultural knowledge permissions, research contributions, teaching and mentoring, dispute repair history, professional development.

The citizen chooses what each viewer sees.

## Sovereign Business Card

A citizen shares one name or QR code. The receiver may access an approved public view containing: name, preferred professional identity, role, organisation, contact route, verified capabilities, services, portfolio, availability, booking, marketplace page, payment options, trust status.

Private information stays hidden.

## Verified Credential Website

Credentials displayed through the domain must show: credential name, issuer, issuer verification, issue date, completion standard, evidence type, accreditation status, expiry date, revocation status, permitted use, verification method.

IN$DEX must distinguish between: participation records, capability credentials, IN$DEX qualifications, professional certifications, government-accredited qualifications, institution-issued qualifications, licences, memberships, community and cultural recognition.

## KYC Credential Layer

The sovereign domain does not abolish KYC. Banks, regulated services, employers, institutions, marketplaces, and authorities may still have legal verification duties. IN$DEX changes how verification is performed.

The current model repeatedly asks citizens to submit: passport, driver licence, address evidence, date of birth, business registration, beneficial ownership, tax information, source-of-funds evidence, photographs, identity checks. This creates friction, duplicated storage, privacy exposure, and identity theft risk.

The IN$DEX model should support reusable verified claims. Examples: identity verified, age requirement satisfied, Cook Islands citizenship verified, New Zealand citizenship verified, address verified, business registration verified, director status verified, beneficial ownership verified, qualification verified, professional licence verified, sanctions screening completed, required transaction checks completed.

The receiving party should receive the minimum proof required. They should not automatically receive every underlying document.

## Verify Once, Prove When Required

Where permitted by law and accepted by the receiving institution: the citizen completes verification, an authorised verifier checks the evidence, IN$DEX creates a signed verification credential, the underlying evidence receives protected handling, the citizen approves each use, the requesting party receives the required proof, the consent is recorded, the citizen sees who accessed the credential, the proof may expire, update, or be revoked.

IN$DEX must never claim one verification satisfies every institution, jurisdiction, or legal duty. Some organisations must perform their own checks. Some credentials require fresh verification. Some records cannot legally be reused. SIINDEX must explain these limits before the citizen proceeds.

## Selective Disclosure System

The citizen should not disclose their full identity record when only one fact is required. Examples: prove the citizen is over 18 without revealing the full date of birth; prove a qualification is valid without exposing unrelated education records; prove a business is registered without displaying private director information publicly; prove residency eligibility without publishing the citizen's home address; prove a licence is current without exposing the original identity documents used to obtain it; prove cultural permission exists without publishing protected cultural knowledge.

Each disclosure must contain: requested information, reason for request, requesting party, legal or operational basis, duration of access, information shared, information withheld, citizen approval, revocation terms, consent receipt.

## Domain View System

The same domain may produce different permissioned views.

- **Public View** — only information approved for public display.
- **Employer View** — qualifications, capabilities, work history, references, and work evidence.
- **Education View** — learning records, prerequisites, enrolment permissions, and verified qualifications.
- **Business View** — registration, products, services, compliance status, business credentials, and transaction routes.
- **Customer View** — products, trust signals, reviews, policies, dispute pathway, and approved contact information.
- **Government or Regulator View** — only records required under lawful authority and defined permissions.
- **Community View** — contribution, local roles, cultural permissions, and community participation.
- **Private Citizen View** — the complete record controlled by the citizen.
- **Emergency View** — a restricted view activated under defined emergency and guardian permissions.

Child citizens require stronger protections and must not receive unrestricted public profiles.

## Credential Source Rule

The citizen domain is the interface. It is not automatically the original source of every fact. Each credential must link to its verified issuer or evidence source.

Examples: a university issues the degree, a TAFE provider issues the qualification, a government authority issues the licence, an employer verifies employment, a customer verifies completed work, a community authority verifies cultural permission.

IN$DEX records, connects, explains, and presents the proof. It must not invent authority it does not hold.

## Citizen-Controlled Reputation

The domain may display verified reputation across different contexts. Reputation must not become one permanent universal score.

A citizen may have separate records for: employment, commerce, mentoring, learning, community contribution, governance, cultural stewardship, project delivery, dispute resolution.

Reputation must include context. A poor marketplace review must not automatically damage access to education, identity, banking, or essential services.

Citizens must receive: right to respond, right to correct factual errors, right to challenge evidence, right to request review, right to show dispute resolution, right to recover after repair, right to export their record, right to leave IN$DEX.

## Sovereign Domain Ownership Record

The citizen must receive a clear record stating: who controls the domain, what rights the citizen holds, whether the domain is transferable, whether renewal is required, what fees apply, what happens after inactivity, what happens after death, what happens after account suspension, how recovery works, how guardianship works, how succession works, how the citizen exports their credentials, what happens if IN$DEX closes.

IN$DEX must not market a domain as permanent ownership if the citizen only receives a renewable licence. The legal nature of the domain must remain clear.

## SIINDEX Domain Guide

SIINDEX helps citizens build and maintain their domain through voice. The citizen speaks. SIINDEX identifies the purpose. SIINDEX asks for missing information. SIINDEX requests permission. SIINDEX verifies the source. SIINDEX builds the correct view. The citizen reviews the result. The citizen approves publication or sharing. IN$DEX records the proof.

**Example:**

> Citizen: SIINDEX, I am applying for an electrical apprenticeship.
>
> SIINDEX: I will prepare an employer view of your sovereign domain. It will include your completed training, safety credentials, verified projects, work availability, and mentor endorsement. Your wallet balance, private identity documents, home address, and unrelated records will remain hidden. Would you like to review the view before sharing it?

## Citizen Domain Safety Rules

No public identity by default. No wallet balance displayed by default. No home address displayed publicly. No full identity document published. No child profile publicly searchable by default. No credential displayed without its status. No unverified claim presented as verified. No reputation score without context. No permanent access without a defined reason. No data sharing without proof of permission or lawful authority. No income, employment, qualification, or investment guarantee. No institution receives more information than required. No citizen loses access to essential records because they leave IN$DEX.

## Citizen Exit Package

When a citizen leaves, IN$DEX must provide: credential export, qualification export, portfolio export, employment record export, business record export, transaction history, consent history, domain content export, reputation evidence, ownership records, marketplace records, dispute records, verification records where legally transferable, instructions for moving to another domain or identity system.

The citizen may lose use of the IN$DEX namespace after departure depending on the legal domain model. They must not lose the underlying evidence they created or lawfully own.

## Institutional Integration

Businesses, TAFE providers, universities, schools, nonprofits, governments, and community organisations may connect to citizen domains as approved credential issuers, verifiers, opportunity providers, or requesters.

Institutions may: issue verified credentials, confirm employment, publish opportunities, request approved information, validate licences, verify project completion, offer training, provide references, sponsor citizens, create apprenticeships, recognise prior learning, support portable records.

Every institutional action must carry a verified institutional identity.

## Strategic Position

A CV tells people what a citizen claims. A sovereign domain shows what trusted parties have verified.

A business card shares contact information. A sovereign domain shares the right information for the right purpose.

KYC repeatedly collects identity documents. A sovereign credential proves only what the transaction requires.

A certificate records one completed course. A sovereign domain records a lifetime of learning, capability, contribution, ownership, and opportunity.

The future citizen does not carry a folder of disconnected proof. The future citizen carries one sovereign address.

`yourname.in$dex`

One name. One trusted gateway. One citizen-controlled record. Many verified possibilities.

## Build status (added by Claude, not part of AJ's original spec)

Nothing in this document is built yet. Against current platform state:

- **Domain claiming** (`web3_domain` column, `.IN$DEX` format) exists today — but only as a flat string per citizen, not the multi-view credential/disclosure system described here.
- **No `credentials`, `consent_disclosures`, `domain_views`, or `institutions` tables exist in Supabase yet.** This spec implies at least those four, plus RLS/RPC design for selective disclosure and revocation.
- **Consent receipt precedent exists** — `consent_receipts` table + RPCs (built this session for transfer/withdraw) is the closest existing pattern to the "consent receipt" concept described here for credential disclosure. Worth reusing/extending rather than building a parallel system.
- **KYC today is Progressive Biometric KYC, Tier 0 only** (phone + liveness scan, no documents) — this spec's "reusable verified claims" (citizenship, business registration, professional licence, etc.) go well beyond what's built or verified today. Any "verified" badge shown to a citizen or institution must be real or explicitly marked otherwise, per this session's standing no-fake-data rule.
- This is a foundational architecture change, not a screen-level fix — per CLAUDE.md Power Phrase #2 ("Implementation Spec — non-trivial build → write spec first, AJ reviews before a line is written"), an implementation spec should be scoped and reviewed before any schema or screen work starts.
