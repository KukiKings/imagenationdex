## Verified historical and cultural facts

**The Cook Islands flag — 15 stars, 15 islands, one circle.** The flag's 15 white five-pointed stars represent the fifteen islands of the Cook Islands (Rarotonga, Aitutaki, Mangaia, Atiu, Mauke, Mitiaro, Manuae, Takutea, Palmerston, Suwarrow, Manihiki, Rakahanga, Pukapuka, Nassau, Tongareva), arranged in a circle specifically to represent unity — many islands, one people. Source: World Atlas / Britannica flag references.

**Melanesia, Micronesia, Polynesia are 1832 colonial inventions, not Indigenous categories.** French naval officer Jules Dumont d'Urville coined the tripartite division of the Pacific in his 1832 paper "Sur les îles du Grand Océan." "Melanesia" was constructed from Greek *melas* (black) + *nēsos* (islands) — explicitly a racial classification based on skin tone, not a division Pacific peoples used for themselves. Before this, Pacific peoples identified by island, clan, language group, and kinship network — not by these externally imposed regional labels. Modern anthropology treats the division as a colonial administrative simplification that obscured real cultural, linguistic, and genetic diversity. Sources: academic sources on Dumont d'Urville's Oceania; ANU Press; multiple historical summaries — independently verified 2026-07-19, consistent with what AJ described.

**Te Moana-nui-a-Kiwa — "the Great Ocean of Kiwa" — is a real, living Polynesian concept of the ocean as ancestor and connector, not divider.** Named for Kiwa, an ancestor-navigator figure appearing across Polynesian oral tradition (Māori mihi, waiata, and oratory invoke the phrase to honor the ancestral voyages that crossed the Pacific). The concept is genuinely about the ocean linking peoples across vast distances through shared ancestry — this is not a marketing reframe, it reflects real Polynesian navigational and oral history. Source: Pacific Collaborative Futures ("Te Moana-nui-a-Kiwa: The Great Connector"), NZ history sources on Pacific voyaging — verified 2026-07-19.

**Vaka (voyaging canoes)** were real vessels of migration, connection, and sovereignty, central to how the Pacific was originally settled and unified before colonization. AJ's earlier "Complete Cook Islands Additions Compilation" document described a specific, current vaka-building/revival project underway in the Cook Islands — that specific claim has not been independently re-verified in this pass (it wasn't part of the searches run 2026-07-19) and should be treated as sourced from that document rather than freshly confirmed.
